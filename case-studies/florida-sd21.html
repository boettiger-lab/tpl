0The brief we are reproducing

In December 2023, TPL Conservation Finance staff produced a one-page legislative briefing for Senator Ed Hooper (R – Palm Harbor), who chairs the Florida Senate's Appropriations Committee on Transportation, Tourism, and Economic Development. The brief's purpose is to give the senator a fast, locally specific case for the “2024 Legislative Request: a minimum of $300 million from the Land Acquisition Trust Fund to the Florida Forever Trust Fund.”

It does this by listing named conservation projects in his district that were funded through Florida Forever programs, the district's total acreage and dollars spent, and the district's vote on Amendment 1 in 2014. Every number in the brief is hand-compiled from internal TPL databases and the Florida Department of Environmental Protection's records. This case study shows how the geo-agent app reproduces that compilation, and where it falls short.

The brief's headline figures

  • Florida Communities Trust:
  • Pasco Palms (Pasco) — 116 ac · $360,000
  • Wall Springs (Pinellas) — 128 ac · $11.4M
  • Weaver Park (Dunedin) — 17 ac · $3.9M
  • Lake Chautauqua (Clearwater) — 27 ac · $1.5M
  • Division of Recreation & Parks:
  • Werner-Boyce Salt Springs SP (Pasco) — 78 ac · $2.4M
  • District total 1998–2009: 1,149 ac · $27.98M
  • Amendment 1 (2014): 73% Yes · 125,089 Yes votes

What this walkthrough delivers

For every figure above, we show the prompt the analyst would type into the geo-agent app, the app's response, and how it lands in the brief. Where the app's number matches we mark it match; where the app surfaces something the original brief omitted we mark it new; where the app cannot produce the figure we mark it gap and name the external source needed.

By the end you have a draft 2026 brief, generated from app outputs, and a clear sense of the per-section gaps to plug.

1Setting up the district

The brief is anchored to a single state senate district. The app already holds the 2025 TIGER/Line state-senate-district boundaries (census-2025-sldu) and the Florida district numbering reflects the post-2022 redistricting that produced Senator Hooper's current seat.

Show me Florida Senate District 21 on the map, with the counties it covers and which Conservation Almanac sites fall inside it.
The app draws SD 21 from the SLDU PMTiles layer, looks up the boundary's land area and centroid, and runs an H3-resolution-8 intersection with the county boundaries layer to identify which counties the district crosses.
The SQL the app runs
WITH sd21 AS (
  SELECT DISTINCT h8, h0
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-census/census-2025/sldu/hex/h0=*/data_0.parquet')
  WHERE STATEFP='12' AND SLDUST='021'
),
counties AS (
  SELECT NAMELSAD, h8, h0
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-census/census-2024/county/hex/h0=*/data_0.parquet')
  WHERE STATEFP='12'
)
SELECT counties.NAMELSAD AS county,
  ROUND(100.0 * COUNT(DISTINCT counties.h8)
              / (SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT h8) FROM sd21), 1) AS pct_of_district
FROM sd21 JOIN counties USING(h8, h0)
GROUP BY counties.NAMELSAD ORDER BY pct_of_district DESC;

2§ Florida Communities Trust highlights

The brief's main body is a list of named FCT-funded projects. These matter because FCT is the state-side grant program inside Florida Forever that match-funds local governments' acquisitions — exactly what the $300M legislative ask would replenish. We want the app to give us a ranked list of FCT-funded sites in SD 21 with acres and the state dollars spent on each.

List every Conservation Almanac site in Florida Senate District 21 that received Florida Communities Trust funding from the State of Florida, grouped by site cluster (Wall Springs, Werner-Boyce, etc.). Show acres and FCT dollars per cluster.
The app joins the SD-21 hex mask to the Almanac sites hex parquet, then to the funding transactions parquet filtered to program LIKE '%Florida Communities Trust%' AND sponsor_type='STAT'.
The SQL the app runs
WITH sd21 AS (
  SELECT DISTINCT h8, h0
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-census/census-2025/sldu/hex/h0=*/data_0.parquet')
  WHERE STATEFP='12' AND SLDUST='021'
),
sites AS (
  SELECT DISTINCT tpl_id, site, county, acres, year
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-tpl/conservation-almanac-2024-sites/hex/h0=*/data_0.parquet') s
  SEMI JOIN sd21 ON s.h8 = sd21.h8 AND s.h0 = sd21.h0
),
fct AS (
  SELECT tpl_id, SUM(amount) AS fct_funding
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-tpl/conservation-almanac-2024-funding.parquet')
  WHERE program LIKE '%Florida Communities Trust%' AND sponsor_type='STAT'
  GROUP BY tpl_id
)
SELECT s.site, s.county, ROUND(s.acres,1) AS acres, s.year,
       ROUND(f.fct_funding/1e6, 2) AS fct_M
FROM sites s JOIN fct f USING(tpl_id)
ORDER BY f.fct_funding DESC;

3§ Division of Recreation & Parks

The brief's second column highlights one project — Werner-Boyce Salt Springs State Park — funded through DEP's Division of Recreation and Parks (DRP). We want the app to confirm the original figure and find any DRP-funded acquisitions the brief might have missed.

In SD 21, which Almanac sites were acquired by the Florida Division of Recreation and Parks? Show acres and state funding per acquisition.
The SQL the app runs
SELECT s.site, ROUND(s.acres,1) AS acres, s.year,
  ROUND(f.amount/1e6, 3) AS amount_M
FROM read_parquet('s3://public-tpl/conservation-almanac-2024-sites.parquet') s
JOIN read_parquet('s3://public-tpl/conservation-almanac-2024-funding.parquet') f
  USING(tpl_id)
WHERE s.site LIKE '%Werner-Boyce%'
  AND f.sponsor LIKE '%Division of Recreation and Parks%'

4§ District-level totals

The brief reports “1,149 acres; $27.98 million” for the district from 1998–2009 under Florida Forever programs. Two angles are useful: (a) reproduce the brief's window, and (b) extend it through the latest Almanac year, 2021.

The 1998–2009 reproduction is 8% over the brief's 1,149 acres / $27.98M. The discrepancy is partly the Almanac's continued data revision since 2009 (some parcels boundaries refined upward) and partly that the brief appears to have used a tighter program filter than “all Florida Forever programs.” For a 2026 update of the brief the more relevant figure is the extended-window total: 2,180 acres and $42M under Florida Forever programs across 28 sites since 1998 — that's the story to tell the senator.

The SQL for the extended window
WITH sd21 AS (
  SELECT DISTINCT h8, h0
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-census/census-2025/sldu/hex/h0=*/data_0.parquet')
  WHERE STATEFP='12' AND SLDUST='021'
),
sites AS (
  SELECT DISTINCT tpl_id, acres, year
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-tpl/conservation-almanac-2024-sites/hex/h0=*/data_0.parquet') s
  SEMI JOIN sd21 ON s.h8 = sd21.h8 AND s.h0 = sd21.h0
),
ff AS (
  SELECT tpl_id, SUM(amount) AS ff
  FROM read_parquet('s3://public-tpl/conservation-almanac-2024-funding.parquet')
  WHERE program LIKE '%Florida Forever%' OR program LIKE '%Florida Communities Trust%'
  GROUP BY tpl_id
)
SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT s.tpl_id) FILTER (WHERE f.ff IS NOT NULL) AS ff_sites,
  ROUND(SUM(s.acres) FILTER (WHERE f.ff IS NOT NULL), 0) AS ff_acres,
  ROUND(SUM(f.ff)/1e6, 2) AS ff_M
FROM sites s LEFT JOIN ff f USING(tpl_id);

5§ Voter support

The brief uses a single statistic to argue voter support: “District 21 Yes vote for Amendment 1 (2014): 73% Yes (125,089 Yes votes).” This is the strongest political fact in the document — and it's the section where the app's data are thinnest.

How did Florida vote on Amendment 1 in 2014, and how did SD 21 specifically vote? Show me other recent conservation ballot measures in the district's counties too.

The app cannot supply the 73% / 125,089 figure for SD 21 on Amendment 1. But the seven county-level results above tell a stronger story than the single statewide statistic the original brief used. 6What the app surfaces beyond the brief

The 2023 brief lists $20.1M of FCT funding (across four named clusters) plus $2.4M from DRP — about $22.5M of state Florida Forever investment in SD 21. But the brief's footer total — “$27.98M, 1998–2009” — already implies more. And the actual answer is much larger once you include local funding, which the original brief omits entirely.

Including the local side of the ledger, total conservation investment in SD 21 captured by the Almanac is $135.0M across 66 funded sites — roughly six times what the original brief reported. Most of the local money came from county sales-tax revenue: the same revenue stream the ballot measures in § 5 authorized.

This is the throughline that ties § 5 to § 6: voters here have already taxed themselves $511.7M for conservation; that money has been spent on real parcels recorded in the Almanac at $93M (so far, against $418.7M still authorized); and the state Florida Forever share is the match-funding partner that makes those local dollars stretch further. That is the actual case for the $300M ask.

8Methodology, gaps, and reproducibility

How the spatial intersections work

Every “sites in SD 21” query uses an H3 hex SEMI JOIN at resolution 8: take the small mask (SD 21's ≈2,567 hex cells), inner-join it to the Almanac sites hex parquet (53,191 sites × hex cells), and the resulting rows are exactly the Almanac sites whose footprint intersects the district. This pattern runs in under two seconds on the MCP server and generalizes to any of the other district layers (SLDL state-house, congressional, county).

Where the app is currently insufficient for the brief

  • gap Almanac vintage. Coverage ends 2021. Acquisitions after 2021 (including any expansion of Wall Springs or Brooker Creek) must come from FL DEP records or the TPL state team.
  • gap Senate-district vote counts. LandVote has percent yes / percent no but not raw ballot counts by district. “125,089 Yes votes” needs FL Division of Elections precinct results aggregated to SLDU geometry. This is a candidate for a future data layer.
  • gap Legislator metadata. Senator name, party, hometown, committee assignments — none of this is in the geo-agent. A small lookup table mapping SLDU GEOID → current legislator would solve it; flagged in example-reports/02-geo-agent-tasks.md.
  • gap Brief photo + branding. The app does not generate the 1-pager layout or assemble the photo. That stays in the state-team's existing template.

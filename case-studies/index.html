What the series demonstrates

Across the three deliverables the geo-agent's contribution ratio declines from ~80% (legislative brief) to ~50% (local feasibility) to ~20% (regional climate-finance study). That's the right shape: the agent's payoff is highest where the spatial intersection IS the deliverable, and lowest where the deliverable is mostly legal mechanism analysis.

The series intentionally walks every prompt — natural-language input in green callouts, the SQL the app runs in expandable disclosures, the gaps where external sources are still needed in orange callouts. The analyst who reads through all three should know exactly which sections of their next report to type into the app first, and which to start with their bond counsel.

Reading order

  1. Case 01 — Florida Senate District 21 sets the format conventions and shows the highest-leverage use case (a 1-pager that the agent essentially generates).
  2. Case 02 — Larimer County, CO shows mid-scale: a multi-section feasibility study where the agent reliably handles the conservation context and the analyst handles the financial/governance/legal context.
  3. Case 03 — Maine North Woods shows the upper limit: a 50+ page mechanism-by-mechanism study where the agent's role is the spatial-data backbone of the first quarter.

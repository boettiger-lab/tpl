TPL Conservation Data Explorer work in progress

An interactive map and AI assistant for exploring land conservation investment, ballot measure history, drinking-water service areas, and environmental data across the United States.

← Open the App

About this project

A research collaboration between the Department of Environmental Science, Policy, and Management at UC Berkeley and the Trust for Public Land, with support from NASA.

This app helps conservation advocates, researchers, and policymakers explore where public dollars and private partnerships have protected land — and which communities, watersheds, and ecosystems still need investment. The interface lets you toggle geospatial layers (protected areas, ballot measures, critical habitat, drinking-water service areas, social vulnerability) and ask natural-language questions that translate into live SQL queries against a federated catalog of cloud-native datasets.

This is a work in progress. Layers, models, and capabilities are being added and refined; a California-specific companion app at tpl-ca.nrp-nautilus.io carries additional state-level layers (CAL FIRE perimeters, CalEnviroScreen, CPAD, WCB projects, etc.).

How it works

The map renders open geospatial datasets directly from cloud-native formats (PMTiles, Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFFs, GeoParquet) hosted on the National Research Platform. No tile server is involved — your browser fetches only the bytes it needs to render the current view.

The AI assistant translates plain-English questions into SQL using DuckDB over the same Parquet files, with column schemas and dataset descriptions discovered automatically through a SpatioTemporal Asset Catalog (STAC).

Low-carbon, open models. The assistant is powered by open-weight large language models (GLM-4.7, Qwen3, GPT-OSS 120B, Kimi K2, MiniMax M2) running on the NRP open-llm-proxy, a shared university inference service running on hardware powered substantially by renewable energy. Compared to large commercial inference services, this approach reduces both the energy and the data-privacy footprint of every conversation. Carbon and energy use are tracked on our companion sustainability dashboard.

The full agent stack — map, chat, MCP tool server, and STAC catalog — is open-source. See the project repository and the geo-agent core library.

Working with the Data Assistant

The Data Assistant doesn't just answer questions — it can drive the map for you. Most of the things people reach for a settings menu to do can be asked in plain language:

  • Adjust layer styling and transparency: "Make Protected Areas 60% transparent", "Color SVI by overall vulnerability", "Shade water service areas by population served."
  • Show, hide, or filter layers: "Turn on Conservation Ballot Measures and turn off PAD-US", "Show only measures that passed since 2010", "Hide everything except Critical Habitat in Florida."
  • Zoom and pan: "Zoom to Congressional District TX-23", "Center the map on the Colorado Front Range."
  • Export query results: "Give me a CSV of community water systems serving more than 100,000 people in California" — the assistant returns a downloadable table.
  • Combine layers in an analysis: "Which protected areas in Oregon fall inside a community water system service area?" — the assistant writes the spatial SQL and shows the result.

And a few interface tips that aren't visually obvious:

  • The Data Assistant panel is resizable — drag its left edge to give chat more or less width.
  • Use the header button to minimize the panel; a floating "show" button restores it.
  • On phones and tablets, the panel auto-collapses to overlay mode so the full map stays visible.
  • PMTiles layers don't use a fixed legend. Hover over any feature for a tooltip with that feature's live attributes — values, names, categories — direct from the source data.
  • Every tool call the assistant runs is logged in the per-turn timeline so you can see exactly what it queried, with what arguments, and what came back.

Data layers & sources

Every layer in the app draws from a public, primary source. Click "STAC" beside any entry to see the full machine-readable metadata — schemas, column descriptions, license, lineage, and download URLs.

Conservation Areas

TPL Conservation Almanac
Trust for Public Land — comprehensive record of conservation acquisitions and easements across the United States, with funding source and program attribution.
STACSource
Conservation Ballot Measures (LandVote)
Trust for Public Land LandVote database — every state and local conservation finance measure put to voters since 1988, with status, finance mechanism, and funds approved.
STACSource
PAD-US 4.1 (Combined)
US Geological Survey Gap Analysis Project — Protected Areas Database of the United States. The combined layer covers fee-owned protected areas, easements, marine areas, and proclamation boundaries with owner, manager, designation, and GAP status.
STACSource
USFWS Critical Habitat — Final & Proposed
US Fish & Wildlife Service — designated critical habitat polygons for ESA-listed species (Final) and proposed designations under public review (Proposed).
STAC: FinalSTAC: ProposedSource

Water Utilities

Community Water System Service Areas (EPA SAB v3)
US Environmental Protection Agency, Office of Research and Development — 44,656 community water-system service-area polygons across all 50 states, DC, tribal, and territorial systems (~99% of CWS-served US population). Each feature carries the EPA PWSID for joins to SDWIS compliance and source-water data. Useful for identifying which utility serves a given location and overlaying service areas with watersheds, protected lands, and wildfire-risk layers for source-water-protection work.
STACSource

Wetlands

Wetland Type (GLWD v2)
Global Lakes and Wetlands Database v2 (Lehner et al.) — 33 wetland classes derived from a global synthesis of inventories and remote sensing.
STACSource

Carbon

Irrecoverable Carbon (2024)
The Nature Conservancy & Conservation International (Noon et al., Nature Sustainability 2022) — ecosystem carbon stocks that, if lost, cannot be recovered by 2050.
STACSource

Land Cover

Copernicus Global Land Cover (CGLS-LC100 2019)
Copernicus Global Land Service v3.0.1 — 100m global land-cover classification (23 classes, FAO LCCS scheme).
STACSource

Population

Population Density (GHS-POP 2020)
Global Human Settlement Layer (European Commission JRC) — 2020 population count at ~90m resolution, R2023A release.
STACSource

Social Vulnerability

Social Vulnerability Index (SVI 2022)
CDC / Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry — county- and tract-level vulnerability composite from 16 census variables.
STACSource

Political Boundaries

Congressional, State Assembly & State Senate Districts
US Census Bureau TIGER/Line — 119th Congress districts (2024) and State Legislative Districts (2025), Upper and Lower chambers, for every state.
STAC: CDSTAC: AssemblySTAC: SenateSource
County Boundaries
US Census Bureau TIGER/Line 2024 — all US county and county-equivalent polygons with FIPS codes.
STACSource
